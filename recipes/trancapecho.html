Trancapecho

Description

The trancapecho or tranca pecho is a type of sandwich originating in Cochabamba (Bolivia), from the Caracota neighborhood. It comes from the silpancho, but differs from it in that it has a toco or tortilla bread. All the ingredients, including the rice and bread, are typical of this city.

In Cochabamba, this dish can be found in the markets.4 The creation of trancapecho is attributed to Ana Camacho, better known as Dona Anita, a vendor at the Las Islas food court, who created the word from conversations with her customers when they consumed the product.

Ingredients

  • 1 bread
  • 1 or 2 steaks for Milanese
  • 1 or 2 fried eggs
  • White rice
  • 1 potato
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 green chili
  • 1/2 green pepper
  • 1/2 red pepper
  • 1/2 onion
  • Breadcrumbs
